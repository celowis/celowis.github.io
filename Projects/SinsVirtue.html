Sins & Virtues

Class: Typography 2: Print
February to March, 2024

Poster Series

“Sins & Virtues” was a project from my Spring 2024 Typography for Print class. The aim was to make eye-catching posters promoting the Sins & Virtues exhibition at the Victoria and Albert Museum. We were tasked with choosing either three sins or virtues to display on our series of posters. My take on the project was to view the virtues in the highest regards, being so pure and sweet and beautiful that it has to be deceiving, being too nice to even look at. With this meaning in mind, I set to visualize it through objects wrapped in cutesy sweet ribbons and sparkles. Each virtue being spelled out through the ribbon itself, meandering in and out of each poster frame. For charity, I chose kitchen utensils and cooking objects to show the giving nature that cooking provides, nourishment to others. The objects chosen for Humility were hair bows, flowers, and makeup, depicting pride in appearance, both humbling one’s unaltered appearance by deeming it unworthy to be left uncovered. I chose soaps and facial serums for purity to show the desperation in a pure appearance and cleanliness I think of when I think of the virtue. With these objects, I set out to arrange them in a way that would bring out their meaning in their respective virtues as well as show how twisted and deceiving they could be. Through the pastel colors of the objects and sparkles, I aimed to show the light innocence in each of these biblical values. To contrast these light colors, I inverted each of the objects and put them against a dark background. Arranged around the frame of each poster, you can see the information for the actual Sins & Virtues exhibition. Included are the website link, V&A logo, as well as the title and dates of the exhibition. All the information uses the same basic framework and structure, yet all are arranged on their posters to best fit their respective compositions.
